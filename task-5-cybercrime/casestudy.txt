Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud has increased rapidly due to the widespread use of digital payment apps.
        A typical fraud begins when the victim receives a call, SMS, or message claiming to be from a bank official, customer care, or even a buyer/seller on online platforms. The fraudster then creates a sense of urgency, such as saying there is a problem with the user’s account or that money needs to be received. Next, they send a “collect request” or a fake payment link and instruct the victim to enter their UPI PIN, falsely claiming it is required to receive money. In reality, entering the PIN authorizes a payment, leading to money being deducted from the victim’s account.
         Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital systems. In some cases, victims may not recover their money.
         This fraud can be prevented by spreading awareness. Users should remember that UPI PIN is only required to send money, never to receive it. Avoid clicking on unknown links and always verify the source before making any transaction
